Q.3 Semantic element in HTML5?

HTML5 introduced several semantic elements that help define the structure of a webpage more meaningfully for browsers.These elements provide clearer indications about the content's purpose and improve accessibility.

1.<header>: Represents introductory content or a group of navigational links typically found at the top of a document or a section.

2. <footer>: Represents the footer of a document or a section, usually containing information about the author, copyright, links to related documents, etc.

3. <nav>: Indicates a section of navigation links, often used to define the navigation menu.

4.<article>: Represents a standalone piece of content, such as a blog post, article, comment, or forum post.

5. <section>: Represents a thematic grouping of content within a document, often used to divide content into different chapters, topics, or sections.

6. <aside>: Represents content tangentially related to the content around it, like sidebars or additional information.

7. <main>: Specifies the main content of a document, excluding any content that is repeated across multiple pages (like headers, footers, or navigation).
