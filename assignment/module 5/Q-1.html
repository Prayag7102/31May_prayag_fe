Q.1 What are the new tags added in HTML5?

Here's the new tags of HTML5

<header>: Defines the header section of a document or a specific section.
<footer>: Defines the footer section of a document or a specific section.
<nav>: Indicates a set of navigation links.
<article>: Represents an independent piece of content within a document, such as a blog post or a news article.
<section>: Defines a thematic grouping within a document.
<aside>: Represents content aside from the main content, like sidebars or related information.
<main>: Specifies the main content within a document.
<figure> and <figcaption>: Used together to embed images, illustrations, diagrams, etc., with a caption.
<video> and <audio>: Tags used to embed video and audio content.
<canvas>: Used for dynamic, scriptable rendering of graphics and images.
<progress>: Indicates the progress of a task.
<details> and <summary>: Used to create a disclosure widget that can be toggled to show/hide additional information.
