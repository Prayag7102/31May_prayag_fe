Q.7 What is === operator?

The === operator, known as the "strong equas to operator," is used in JavaScript for comparison. It checks for equality between two values while also checking if their types are the same.

Example:

5 === 5 evaluates to true because both operands are of the same value (5) and type (number).
5 === '5' evaluates to false because even though the values are the same, their types (number and string) are different.
